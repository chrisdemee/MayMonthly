About the Team

The Buffalo Bills were established in 1960 as a charter member of the American Football League (AFL). They joined the NFL in the AFL-NFL merger of 1970.

They are the only team to play home games in New York State (in Orchard Park, near Buffalo).

The Bills are 4 time AFC champions and were known for the “K-Gun” offense with Jim Kelly and Thurman Thomas.

Bills fans are some of the most loyal (and rowdiest) in all of football

Bills are the only NFL team to play in the snow regularly, making their home games famous for harsh winter conditions.

They are known for their famous "Wide Right" playoff misses in the early 1990s, a heartbreak for fans during crucial games.

The Bills have appeared in four consecutive Super Bowls (1990–1993), a record unmatched by any other NFL team.

The team's mascot, “Billy Buffalo,” is a beloved figure at games, rallying fans and entertaining crowds.
